# LinkedIn Sales Navigator

**Type**: Account + contact database (premium)
**Available via**: Clay-managed OR own key (Sales Nav seat)
**Best for**: Highest-quality contact data; enterprise targets; LinkedIn URL verification

---

## What It Returns

| Data Point | Coverage | Confidence |
|------------|----------|------------|
| Contact list at company | 90% | Very high |
| Title (LinkedIn-verified) | 99% | Very high |
| LinkedIn URL | 95% | Very high |
| Employment history | 95% | Very high |
| Person seniority + function | 95% | Very high |

## Cost

| Operation | Clay-Managed | Own Key (Seat) |
|-----------|--------------|-----------------|
| Search + return contacts | 2 credits per contact | 0 (own seat) |
| LinkedIn URL discovery | 2 credits | 0 |

## When to Use

- ✅ Enterprise / Fortune 500 targets
- ✅ T1 ABM where contact quality matters
- ✅ When LinkedIn URL is critical (DM steps in cadence)
- ✅ When Apollo / Findymail under-perform
- ✅ When you already have a Sales Nav seat (use own key)

## When NOT to Use

- ❌ SMB lists (Apollo coverage is comparable, cheaper)
- ❌ High-volume runs without own key (2 credits per contact adds up fast)
- ❌ Email discovery (Sales Nav doesn't return emails — pair with waterfall)

## Notes

- Sales Nav is the gold standard for contact accuracy. LinkedIn data is fresher than any third-party database.
- Own key path: connect via Clay's LinkedIn integration → uses your seat's search capacity.
- Search URL inputs: paste a Sales Nav search URL into Clay's source — Clay handles pagination.

## Common Issues

| Symptom | Cause | Fix |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Limited search results | Sales Nav search filters too narrow | Broaden filters or use multiple search URLs |
| Slow enrichment | LinkedIn rate limits per-seat | Throttle or distribute across multiple seats |
| Missing email | Expected — Sales Nav doesn't provide email | Pair with email waterfall |
